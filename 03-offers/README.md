# 03 — Offers

Where diagnostic findings become concrete offers: a clear promise, a clear
buyer, a clear price, a clear delivery.

**Offers in this folder are downstream from diagnostics.** An offer is not
drafted because a type of work seems appealing — it is drafted because
diagnostic briefs and discovery conversations have revealed a specific,
recurring problem that buyers care about and are positioned to pay to fix.

## Subfolders

- `opportunities/` — raw ideas under evaluation. Graduate to `drafts/` once
  supported by diagnostic signal, or kill them.
- `drafts/` — offers being shaped. Each draft should state: who it's for,
  outcome, scope, price, delivery, and what diagnostic evidence supports it.
- `active/` — offers currently in market. Only move here once there is
  confirmed buyer interest (not just hypothesis).

## Standard structure for an offer file

```
# <Offer name>

- Status: opportunity | draft | active | retired
- ICP (who):
- Diagnostic basis: [what patterns or signals this offer responds to]
- Outcome (what they get):
- Scope (what's included / excluded):
- Price & structure:
- Delivery timeline:
- Proof required to sell it:
- Risks / unknowns:
```

## Rules

- Do not create offer drafts before diagnostic patterns support a specific
  intervention type and ICP.
- Do not graduate to `active/` before at least one discovery conversation
  confirms buyer interest.
- Per-engagement offers are normal at this stage. Every engagement does not
  need to fit a predefined package.
- Retired offers move to `99-archive/offers/` with a short post-mortem.
